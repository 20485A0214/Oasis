SUBASH CHANDRA BOSE

About Us :

Subhas Chandra Bose (also spelled Subhash Chandra Bose) was an Indian nationalist leader and one of the most prominent figures in the struggle for India's independence from British rule. He was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, India, and he died under controversial circumstances on August 18, 1945.

Here are some key points about Subhas Chandra Bose's life and contributions:

Early Life and Education:

Subhas Chandra Bose came from a well-educated and well-off family. He completed his education in India and later went to England to study at Cambridge University. However, his nationalist sentiments led him to leave his studies and return to India to actively participate in the freedom movement.

Formation of Forward Bloc:

In 1939, Bose was elected the president of the Indian National Congress. However, due to differences with the Gandhian leadership, he resigned and formed a new political party called the Forward Bloc. This marked a shift towards more direct and forceful actions against British rule.

Indian National Congress:

Bose became an active member of the Indian National Congress (INC) and was deeply influenced by Mahatma Gandhi's nonviolent methods. However, he eventually became disillusioned with the INC's moderate approach and sought more radical means to achieve India's independence.

Escape to Germany and Japan:

During World War II, Bose believed that India's opportunity for independence lay in taking advantage of the conflict between the Allies and the Axis powers. He escaped house arrest in India and traveled to Germany and later to Japan to seek their support for India's freedom struggle.

Indian National Army (INA):

In collaboration with the Japanese, Bose established the Indian National Army (INA) or Azad Hind Fauj, comprising Indian prisoners of war and civilians in Southeast Asia. The INA aimed to fight alongside the Axis powers against British colonialism. Bose was given the title "Netaji" (Respected Leader) by his followers.
